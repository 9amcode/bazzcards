## Sales Script

### Step 1 — Opening (at reception)
> "Good morning. My name is [Name] from Bazzcards. We work with companies across Kampala to upgrade how their teams share contact information. I'd love five minutes with whoever manages your staff onboarding or admin — is that person available today?"

*If busy:* "No problem at all. May I leave a card and follow up? What's the best time to come back?"

### Step 2 — The Hook (first 60 seconds with decision maker)
> "Thank you for your time. Quick question — when one of your staff meets a client or partner and shares their contact, what do they use right now?"

*After they answer:* "That's exactly why we exist. Bazzcards gives every employee a smart card — one tap and their full contact, title, and company profile saves directly to the other person's phone. No app needed. Let me show you."

### Step 3 — The Demo
> "May I? Just tap this on your phone."

*After tap:* "That's what happens every time one of your staff meets someone. Instant. Professional. And you never run out."

### Step 4 — The Offer
> "For a team your size, we'd be looking at [X] cards. That comes to [Y] per card, with your company branding on every card. We handle design, delivery, and setup. How many people are currently on your team?"

### Step 5 — The Close
> "We can have your cards designed and delivered within [X] days. To get started I just need a purchase order or 50% deposit, and your company logo. What would be the easiest way to move this forward from your end?"

*Don't fill the silence. Let them respond.*

## Handling Objections

### "We already use paper business cards."
> "Exactly — most companies do. The problem with paper cards is they run out, get lost, and go straight in the bin. With Bazzcards, one card works forever, you never reprint, and when a staff member changes title or number you update it in seconds — no cost. The card your team uses today could still be working in three years."

**Then ask:** "How much does your company spend on reprinting cards each year?"

### "This isn't in our budget right now."
> "I understand. Can I ask — when does your next budget cycle start? Because the companies seeing the most value are locking in the annual rate now, which gives them two months free. I can give you a formal quote today so your team has something to work with for the next approval."

**Then ask:** "Who else would need to sign off on this besides yourself?"

### "Let me think about it / I'll get back to you."
> "Of course. Can I ask what the main thing is you'd want to think through? I want to make sure I've given you everything you need."

*If vague:* "No problem. Let me send you a one-page summary and our pricing on WhatsApp right now — that way you have it ready. What's the best number?"

*Then set a specific follow-up:* "I'll check back in on [specific day]. Does morning or afternoon work better?"

## Demo Flow

1. **Walk in confident, ask for the right person** — Don't pitch at reception. Ask for HR Manager, Admin Manager, or whoever handles staff onboarding.
2. **Open with a question, not a pitch** — "When your staff meet clients, how do they share their contact details right now?"
3. **Do the tap — say nothing** — Hand them the card. Don't explain before the tap. Watch their face.
4. **Connect it to their team** — "Imagine every one of your [X] staff doing that in every meeting. One card, forever."
5. **Give the number, then be quiet** — State the price clearly. Don't apologise. Stop talking.
6. **Leave with a next step — always** — A follow-up time, a WhatsApp number, a name to come back for.

## Key Tips

- Send the same-day WhatsApp within **2 hours** of leaving the office. Response rates drop significantly after 24 hours.
- The **free design mockup offer** is the strongest closing tool. It moves them from "thinking about it" to seeing their actual brand on the card. Always make this offer.
- **Never leave without a next step** — a follow-up time, a WhatsApp number, or a name to ask for.
